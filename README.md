# Tech-Giants-Analysis


## Project Overview 
This project is investigates the relationship between company size (measured by full_time_employment) and operaional efficiency (measured by revenue and net income per employee)
for majot technology corporations. our goal is to determine if large-scale human capital is a stategic advantage or an operational burden.

## Key Insights
After Analyzing the financial data of major tech companies, we found the following result:
= We discovered a strong negative correlation (-0.684) between company size and revenue per employee. This suggests that as these companies grow and hire
more people, the average productivity per employee ends to decline.

= Our finding indicates that larger companies may struggle to maintain the same level of efficiency as smaller ones,
increased management complexity and administrative costs often outweigh the benefifts of a larger workforce.

= We usee a T-Test to confirm our results. The analysis showed a P-value of 0.59 is higher than 0.05 so their is no statistically significant difference between the two groups
compared in terns of efficiency.
we failed to reject the null hypothesis

= We also use Chi-Square and the P-value equal 0.0036 This confirmed a relationship between employment types and efffciency levels within the studied companies.

= By performing a VIF(Variance Inflation Factor) analysis we identified high multicollinearity between Total Revenue and Gross Profit this indicates that both features carry 
similar information 

our analysis revealed a significant variation in employee productivity across different tech giants, demonstrating that increasing headcount does not always guarantee higher operational efficiency,also
companies should focus more on how productive each employee is , rather than just trying to hire more people the most successful companies are those that use technology to grow and make more money without needing to hire a huge number of extra staff.
